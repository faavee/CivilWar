# House Divided Study Guide

## Introduction
The House Divided period was marked by significant tensions leading up to the Civil War. This study guide covers essential topics and provides detailed answers to review questions.

### Key Topics
1. **The Concept of a House Divided**
   - Explanation of the phrase used by Abraham Lincoln.
   - Context within the political landscape.

2. **Major Events Leading to the Civil War**
   - Kansas-Nebraska Act.
   - Dred Scott Decision.
   - John Brown's Raid.

3. **Key Figures**
   - Abraham Lincoln.
   - Stephen Douglas.
   - Harriet Tubman and the Underground Railroad.

4. **Social and Economic Factors**
   - Southern economy vs. Northern industrialization.
   - The role of slavery in society.

5. **Review Questions**
   - What does a "house divided" mean and how does it relate to national unity?
   - Discuss the impacts of the Dred Scott Decision.
   - Evaluate the significance of the Kansas-Nebraska Act in increasing tensions.

## Detailed Answers
1. **What does a "house divided" mean and how does it relate to national unity?**
   - Lincoln emphasized that the nation could not endure permanently half slave and half free, leading to disunion.

2. **Discuss the impacts of the Dred Scott Decision.**
   - This decision heightened sectional tensions and negated Congressional authority over slavery in the territories.

3. **Evaluate the significance of the Kansas-Nebraska Act in increasing tensions.**
   - It allowed for popular sovereignty, which led to violence and conflict in Kansas, termed "Bleeding Kansas."
